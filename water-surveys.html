The Importance of Water Surveys

A hydrogeological survey is a critical first step before any drilling project. It involves a scientific study of underground water conditions to locate viable aquifers. This minimizes the risk of a dry borehole, saving you time and money.

Precision Targeting

Pinpoint the best drilling location with scientific accuracy.

Risk Reduction

Avoid the costs and frustration of drilling a dry well.

Aquifer Analysis

Understand the depth, yield, and quality of the water source.

Regulatory Compliance

Ensure your project meets all local and national water authority standards.

Our Survey Process

1

Initial Consultation

We start by understanding your needs and reviewing topographical maps and existing data of your area.

2

Geophysical Survey

Our team conducts on-site resistivity tests to measure how the ground resists electricity, indicating where water may be present.

3

Data Analysis

We analyze the collected data to model the underground geological structure and identify potential water-bearing fractures.

4

Reporting & Recommendation

You receive a detailed report with our findings, pinpointing the optimal drilling spot and estimated depth.
